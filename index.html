Weird-ass controllers

The game supports multiple controllers but the weirder ones where made by Namco itself...

From top to bottom the JoGcon and the NeGcon

NEGCON

Released in 1995 as a way to accurately control cars in Namco's racing games.
The controller is separated in two halves by a swivel-joint which allows the player to twist them, That movement is then translated to move left or right

The L and R buttons as well as Square and X (labeled I and II) are analog buttons allowing for more finer control of the accelerator and brake.
It has support for most of the racing games on the PS1 Library
Weirdly enough it's official webpage is still up

It twists and turns
Pretty long analog buttons
Surprisingly comfortable and easy to get the hang on

JOGCON

Parallel to developing this game, Namco released a new controller with it.
The dial in the middle uses motors to provide force feedback, but the dial it's hard to hold and in my experience sometimes goes crazy giving feedback.
It was sold as a bundle with the game as well as separate.
In the end it didn't do great as evidenced by it only being supported fully in 5 games:

  • Ridge Racer Type 4
  • V-Rally 2
  • Pac-Man World
  • Breakout
  • Ridge Racer V

The pocketstation game allowed you to see which cars you had unlocked as well as trade them with other players.
Apart from that... nothing really too exciting, just ID numbers and team names!

Rocket Start

It's a technique available when using a car with Manual Transmission(MT)
On the start countdown hold down the accelerator and shift up to the last gear while keeping the revs between 6k and 7k, the last shift should coincide with the race starting.
You'll get a boost to the max speed of the car and see the 8th and 7th car rubberbanding like crazy.
