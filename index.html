Weird-ass controllers

The game supports multiple controllers but the weirder ones where made by Namco itself...

NEGCON

Released in 1995 as a way to accurately control cars in Namco's racing games.
The controller is separated in two halves by a swivel-joint which allows the player to twist them. That movement is registered to make the cars turn left or right
Also the L and R buttons as well as Square and X (Labeled I and II) are analog buttons allowing for more finer control of the accelerator and brake.
It has support for most of the racing games on the PS1 Library Weirdly enough it's official webpage is still up

JOGCON

Parallel to developing this game, Namco released a new controller with it.
The dial in the middle uses motors to provide force feedback, but it's hard to reach and in my experience sometimes stops giving feedback altogether.
It was sold as a bundle with the game as well as separate.
In the end it didn't do great as evidenced by it only being supported fully in 5 games:

  • Ridge Racer Type 4
  • V-Rally 2
  • Pac-Man World
  • Breakout
  • Ridge Racer V

These two controllers are valuable in a speedrun setting because you don't lose as much speed while turning

HI-Spec Demo

The original release includes a playable demo of Ridge Racer 1 running at 60 fps (50 on PAL land) as a way to show how far acquainted they've become with the hardware and as a statement for future games.

PocketStation support

The pocketstation game allowed you to see which cars you had unlocked as well as trade them with other players.

Rocket Start

It's a technique available when using a car with Manual Transmission(MT)
On the start countdown hold down the accelerator and shift up to the last gear while keeping the revs between 6k and 7k, the last shift should coincide with the race starting.
You'll get a boost to the max speed of the car and see the 8th and 7th car rubberbanding like crazy.

Motion blur

On replays press Triangle to activate motion blur

Racing An Eagle

In Grand Prix, On Crimsonrock Pass and during countdown at the beginning of the race press Left, Right 3 times

The case of the many Reikos

First introduced in the attract mode of Rave Racer(1995), Reiko Nagase became a mainstay of the series. The success of Type 4 spawned a bunch of Reiko look alikes in other Namco series, mainly:

- Rena Hirose - Rena Hayami (Ace combat 3) - Kei Nagase (Ace combat 2/5/7)
